Challenges

Residents and workers alike encounter various hurdles when navigating the waste management landscape. Understanding these challenges is crucial to enhancing the overall experience and ensuring the efficiency and safety of waste disposal processes:

For Residents:

  • Limited Slot Availability: Securing preferred time slots can be challenging due to limited availability, leading to scheduling difficulties for residents.
  • Complex Booking Procedures: Council websites often feature complex and cumbersome booking processes, resulting in frustration and errors for users.
  • Access to Information: Difficulty accessing necessary information about HWRC locations and the services they offer adds another layer of challenge for residents.

For Workers:

  • Visitor Flow Management: Maintaining a smooth visitor flow and ensuring compliance with safety guidelines, particularly during peak times, poses a significant challenge for HWRC workers.
  • Overcrowding and Wait Times: Dealing with overcrowding and long wait times can impact the efficiency and safety of waste management operations at HWRCs.
  • Addressing Queries and Concerns: Workers must address queries and concerns from residents regarding booking procedures, site regulations, and other related issues, adding to their workload.

By recognizing and addressing these challenges, Veolia is committed to making waste management more efficient, accessible, and sustainable for both residents and workers.

COVID-19 Safety Guidelines

Review our safety guidelines before visiting our HWRCs.

Guidelines:

  • Maintain social distancing of at least 2 meters (6 feet) from others at all times.
  • Wear a face covering or mask while on-site, unless exempted for medical reasons.
  • Use hand sanitizing stations provided before and after handling waste materials.
  • Follow directional signage and staff instructions to navigate the site safely.
  • Limit the number of individuals from your household visiting the HWRC to essential personnel only.
  • Stay home if you or anyone in your household is experiencing symptoms of COVID-19 or is required to self-isolate.

Precautions:

  • Regularly clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces, such as door handles
  • Avoid touching your face, eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands.
  • Use contactless payment methods whenever possible to minimize physical contact.
  • Dispose of any used personal protective equipment (PPE), such as gloves or masks, in designated bins provided on-site.
  • If using reusable containers for waste disposal, ensure they are thoroughly cleaned and sanitized before and after each visit.
  • Be patient and respectful towards staff and other visitors, as they work to maintain a safe environment for everyone.
